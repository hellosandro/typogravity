From SVG glyph to Matter.js collision — the three things to know

  • 1. Bodies must be convex. Most letters are concave, so the collision shape is a compound of several convex pieces.
  • 2. Holes (counters) aren't subtracted — they're the gap. You never cut a hole into a body; you place convex pieces around the empty area, and that empty area becomes the counter.
  • 3. Curves become straight pieces. A round counter (O) needs many small convex segments — more segments = smoother, but more bodies.

O — glyph (SVG path)

In SVG the hole is trivial: one <path> with fill-rule="evenodd" and an inner sub-loop. This is the visual.

O — collision (convex pieces)

The ring is sliced into 14 convex quads. None covers the center — so the counter exists purely as the uncovered gap. (Matter can't use the ring as one body.)

A — glyph (3 solids merged)

A's strokes are straight, so the same 3 convex pieces, filled solid in one colour, already are the letter. The triangular counter is just the gap between them.

A — collision (convex pieces)

Two leg parallelograms + one crossbar rectangle — all convex. The counter is, again, only the empty space no piece covers.
